Introduction

Our Wheeled Quadrupedal Manipulator (WQM) platform combines the LimX W1, a 16-DOF wheeled quadruped, and a 6-DOF airbot arm. Both components are efficiently powered by the W1's battery system. The platform utilizes a unified control policy, meticulously trained and deployed to achieve precise omni-directional task space pose tracking. The positioning of the end-effector is precisely estimated through a combination of Lidar Inertial Odometry (LIO) and forward kinematics. Framework

The following figure shows the training paradigm for our whole-body policy. The rewards integrated by the Reward Fusion Module generate a reasonable return. With an approximated value function, the Generalized Advantage Estimator computes the advantage for the PPO algorithm update. The unified policy takes the 6D target pose (R,p) from the user, latent \hat{z} encoded from the privilege encoder, and proprioceptive states.
